About Ethiopian National Blood Bank

The Ethiopian National Blood Bank is the country's central hub for blood collection, testing, processing, and distribution. Established in 2005, we serve over 120 hospitals nationwide.

Our mission is to ensure a safe, adequate, and sustainable blood supply for all Ethiopians in need, while maintaining the highest standards of quality and safety.

Ready to Donate?

Eligibility Requirements

  • Age between 18-65 years
  • Weight at least 50 kg (110 lbs)
  • Good general health
  • No tattoos/piercings in last 6 months
  • Not pregnant or breastfeeding

Before You Donate

  • Get a good night's sleep
  • Eat iron-rich foods (meat, fish, beans)
  • Drink extra fluids (water, juice)
  • Bring ID with name and photo
  • Wear comfortable clothing

Frequently Asked Questions

Generally, you can donate blood if you:

  • Are between 18-65 years old
  • Weigh at least 50 kg (110 lbs)
  • Are in good health
  • Have not donated blood in the last 3 months
  • Have no recent tattoos or piercings (within 6 months)
  • Are not pregnant or breastfeeding

Men can donate blood every 3 months (4 times per year), while women can donate every 4 months (3 times per year). This allows your body time to replenish the donated red blood cells.

Yes, blood donation is extremely safe. We use sterile, single-use equipment for each donor. The most common side effects are slight dizziness or bruising at the needle site, which typically resolve quickly.

The entire process takes about 45 minutes to 1 hour, including registration, health screening, donation, and refreshments. The actual blood donation only takes 8-10 minutes.

After donation, your blood is:

  • Tested for infectious diseases
  • Processed into components (red cells, plasma, platelets)
  • Stored under strict conditions
  • Distributed to hospitals as needed
  • Used to save lives within 42 days (red cells) or up to 1 year (plasma)
